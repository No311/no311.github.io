# Professional 
## AuDaLa
<!-- ## [AuDaLa]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link projects/AuDaLa.md %}) -->

*Current status: Active, secondary*

During my PhD, I created the basis for a parallel programming language called AuDaLa (Autonomous Data Language), working together with my supervisors, [Thomas Neele](tneele.com) and [Jan Friso Groote](https://www.tue.nl/en/research/researchers/jan-friso-groote), as well as Gijs Leemrijse and the members of the AVVA project. 
The fruits of this labour can be found in my thesis, titled [A Parallel Programming Perspective - Design of AuDaLa and AuDaLogic]({{ site.url }}/download/A_Parallel_Programming_Perspective.pdf).

The goal of AuDaLa is to facilitate understandable, usable and verifiable parallel programming.
The thesis gives a solid theoretical grounding of AuDaLa, as well as examples, adaptations and methods to verify the language to achieve these goals.
To some extent, this is achieved, but as can also be read in the thesis, much work can still be done (and one or two mistakes in some methods can still be ironed out).

This leads to the following two next steps:
1. AuDaLa should evolve from academic mostly theoretical programming language to a full-fledged programming language. 
2. The verification methods of AuDaLa should be further developed and proven correct. For this, I envision to formalize AuDaLa and its verification methods in [Rocq](https://rocq-prover.org/) or [Iris](https://iris-project.org/).

If you are interested in contributing, drop me a mail.
